## Concept

**Tagline:** From a lonely dev with a laptop to the world's most famous solopreneur — as fast as possible.

You play as an indie developer trying to build a SaaS product, accumulate wealth, and retire as the richest solopreneur in history. The game is an **incremental with a hard win condition**: accumulate $1B in total money and hit "Retire."

What makes it different from typical idle games: it is **designed to be speedrun**. The first playthrough is a discovery experience where powerful mechanics unlock progressively. Subsequent runs start fresh but the *player* carries full knowledge, enabling tighter strategies and faster completions. A timer is always visible. Finished runs are recorded on a personal dashboard.

---

## Theme & Narrative

You are a developer. You open your laptop. You start writing code.

The world is the modern SaaS ecosystem: shipping features, acquiring users, fighting churn, posting on social media, and progressively delegating everything to AI models so you can focus on strategy. No employees — just you, your product, and an ever-growing fleet of AI agents.

The tone is dry and self-aware: upgrade names, flavor text, and milestone messages should wink at real solopreneur culture (indie hackers, "build in public", MRR screenshots, etc.).

---

## Win Condition

**Retire at $1,000,000,000 (total money).**

Money is earned through freelance missions and SaaS subscriptions. It accumulates in the player's wallet. Spending money on investments accelerates growth but delays the win — creating the core tension of every run.

When the player reaches $1B, a "Retire" button appears. Clicking it ends the run, records the elapsed time, and posts the result to the personal speedrun dashboard.

## Core Resource Loop

```
[Click] → RCUs
              ├──→ Freelance Missions tab  → 💰 Money (direct, early game)
              └──→ Ship Feature tab        → SaaS Product properties ↑
                                                    │
                                         Customers × Subscription Price
                                                    │
                                               Monthly 💰 Money
                                                    │
                                          Investment tab (optional spend)
                                                    │
                                              Growth boosts
                                                    ↓
                                              $1B → Retire
```

### Resources

**RCUs (Raw Code Units)** — the primary craftable resource. Generated by clicking "Write Code" or later by AI models. Spent in two places: Freelance Missions and Ship Feature.

**Money** — the win resource. Accumulated from freelance work and SaaS MRR. Spent in the Investment tab for time-limited boosts. The core tension: spend now for faster growth, or hold for the $1B goal.

**Reputation** — a passive multiplier earned through slow, deliberate marketing actions. Boosts the SaaS product's Marketing Stream.

---

## The SaaS Product

The product is the player's core passive income engine. It has three properties that drive how many customers it acquires and keeps:

| Property | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Customer Satisfaction** | Multiplier on the conversion rate: how likely a potential client becomes a paying customer. |
| **Customer Retention** | Multiplier on the monthly renewal rate: how likely a customer stays after their monthly period ends. |
| **Marketing Stream** | The number of potential clients that "visit" the product each in-game day. |

The product also has a **Subscription Price**, which is a fixed property (not adjustable by the player — it's part of the product's identity). Price may evolve through mid-game upgrades or milestone unlocks.

Each month, the game resolves:
```
New customers = Marketing Stream × conversion_rate(Customer Satisfaction)
Churned customers = existing customers × churn_rate(Customer Retention)
Monthly revenue = active customers × Subscription Price
```

## Tabs

The UI is organized around tabs. Each tab is the player's primary decision space at a given stage of the game.

### Tab 1 — Write Code
The home base. A single "Write Code" button generates RCUs on click. Later, AI coders generate RCUs passively and this tab becomes a management panel for AI models.

RCU counter and current RCU/s rate always visible here.

### Tab 2 — Ship Feature
Spend RCUs to improve the SaaS product's properties.

Each "feature" is a one-time or stackable upgrade that permanently increases Customer Satisfaction, Customer Retention, or Marketing Stream. Features have increasing RCU costs (standard incremental curve). 

This tab is the long-game investment: money doesn't flow directly from here, but the compounding effect on MRR is massive.

### Tab 3 — Freelance Missions
Three missions available per in-game day, each displaying:
- A flavor name (e.g., "Fix a client's WordPress site", "Build a landing page")
- RCU cost
- Money reward

Once accepted and paid, money is added instantly. Missions refresh daily. This is the player's primary source of early-game money before MRR scales up.

#### Developer tiers

Missions scale based on **total lifetime RCUs generated** — a measure of the player's cumulative coding experience, not their wealth. Four tiers unlock progressively:

| Tier | Unlock (total RCUs) | Mission flavor | RCU cost range | Payout range |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Junior** | 0 (default) | Fix bugs, build landing pages, WordPress | Low | Low |
| **Senior** | TBD | Build features, integrate APIs, write SDKs | Medium | Medium |
| **Lead** | TBD | Architect systems, audit codebases, solo sprints | High | High |
| **10x** | TBD | Build an MVP in a weekend, rescue a dying startup | Very high | Very high |

Exact RCU thresholds to be tuned during balancing. The tiers should feel like a natural career arc — Junior missions become embarrassingly easy by the time Senior unlocks, which signals the player that they've grown.

Higher tiers don't replace lower ones: the three daily missions are drawn from the player's current tier, keeping the tab relevant and the payout meaningful throughout the run.

### Tab 4 — Investment
Spend money on time-limited boosts and one-off growth events. This tab introduces the spend-vs-hold tension central to the speedrun meta.

| Investment | Effect | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| **Cold Outreach campaign** | Temporary spike in Marketing Stream | 1 in-game day |
| **SEO push** | Gradual Marketing Stream boost | 7 in-game days |
| **Sponsored newsletter** | Flat Reputation gain | Instant |
| **Launch on Product Hunt** | Massive one-time Marketing Stream + Reputation event | Instant (once per run) |
| **Press coverage** | Large Reputation spike | Instant (limited uses) |

More investments unlock at money milestones.

### Tab 5 — AI Agents

Unlocked fairly early in the run. This is where the player hires, configures, and upgrades AI models that automate parts of the game loop.

#### The Frontier Lab

All AI agents are sourced from a single fictional vendor: **The Frontier Lab** — a shadowy AI company with suspiciously good models and suspiciously aggressive pricing. Flavor text should lean into the Faustian bargain angle: you're getting immense leverage, but it costs you.

Each agent is a product sold by The Frontier Lab under a subscription model. The player doesn't set a raw spend amount — they choose a **subscription plan** for each agent. Plans are stepped tiers with fixed daily costs and fixed multipliers. The player can scroll up or down through tiers at any time to upgrade or downgrade dynamically.

#### Agent lifecycle

**1. Unlock** — pay a one-time RCU cost to access an agent's listing in The Frontier Lab. Once unlocked, the agent starts on the Free tier.

**2. Subscribe** — scroll through the agent's plan tiers to pick a subscription level. The daily cost is automatically deducted from the player's money each in-game day. Scrolling up upgrades, scrolling down downgrades. No confirmation needed — switching is instant and encouraged.

**3. Upgrade** — separately from the plan tier, spend RCUs to upgrade the agent's model version. Each model upgrade increases the multiplier at every plan tier. Think of it as upgrading from GPT-3 to GPT-4 to GPT-o1 — same pricing structure, noticeably better output.

#### Subscription tiers (same structure for all agents)

| Plan | Daily cost | Multiplier |
|---|---|---|
| **Free** | $0 | — (agent idle) |
| **Hobbyist** | $5/day | 1.5× |
| **Growth** | $25/day | 4× |
| **Scale** | $100/day | 12× |
| **Infernal** | $500/day | 40× |

Multipliers stack with model upgrades:
```
effective_boost = plan_multiplier × model_multiplier(upgrade_tier)
```

#### Core agents

| Agent | Unlock cost (RCUs) | Boosts |
|---|---|---|
| **AI Coder** | Low (first unlockable) | RCU/s generation rate |
| **AI Support** | Medium | Customer Retention (reduces churn) |
| **AI Marketer** | Medium-high | Marketing Stream + Reputation gain |

Additional agents unlock at later milestones (see Progression Milestones).

#### Strategic tension

The Frontier Lab drains money every in-game day. Running agents on high-tier plans accelerates compounding but bleeds the wallet. Near $1B, the optimal play is often to downgrade all agents to Free to stop the daily drain and let savings accumulate to the finish line. Knowing *when* to throttle is a core speedrun skill.

Model upgrades (RCU cost) vs. SaaS feature shipping (also RCU cost) remains the other key routing competition.

---

### Tab 6 — Post on X *(passive, always visible)*
A dedicated action with a cooldown timer (e.g., 5 real-time minutes). Clicking posts a "thread" and grants a Reputation boost. Not a core loop mechanic — more of a background habit the player can maintain for marginal gain.

## Early Game Mechanic Flow

1. Player clicks **Write Code** → accumulates RCUs
2. Player opens **Freelance Missions** → spends RCUs, earns first money
3. Player opens **Ship Feature** → spends RCUs to improve the product; product starts generating small MRR
4. **AI Agents tab unlocks early** → player pays RCUs to unlock AI Coder; sets a modest daily spend → RCU generation goes passive
5. Player now manages three RCU sinks simultaneously: Freelance (cash), Ship Feature (MRR), Agent upgrades (efficiency)
6. Money starts flowing from both freelance and MRR → player sets daily Agent spend higher
7. First Investment becomes available → first meaningful money spend decision (boost vs. save)
8. AI Support and AI Marketer unlock → churn drops, Marketing Stream grows, MRR compounds faster
9. Player shifts from active clicking to strategic configuration

## Progression Milestones

Two separate milestone tracks gate different unlocks.

**RCU milestones** (total lifetime RCUs generated):

| Total RCUs | Unlock |
|---|---|
| 50 | AI Coder available to unlock (Agents tab) |
| TBD | Freelance tier: **Senior** |
| TBD | Freelance tier: **Lead** |
| TBD | Freelance tier: **10x** |

**Money milestones** (total money accumulated):

| Money | Unlock |
|---|---|
| $100 | **AI Agents tab unlocks** |
| $1K | AI Support Agent available to unlock |
| $2K | AI Marketer available to unlock |
| $5K | Product Hunt launch available (Investment tab) |
| $25K | AI Agent: Growth Hacker — boosts Marketing Stream conversion rate |
| $50K | Enterprise subscription tier unlocked |
| $100K | AI Agent: PR Bot — generates press events passively |
| $500K | AI Agent: Product Manager — auto-ships Ship Feature upgrades |
| $1M | Growth loops unlocked |
| $10M | AI CEO mode — all agents run at maximum, player focuses on investment routing |
| $1B | **"Retire" button appears** |

## Speedrun Meta

### How it works
- Each run starts from zero: no RCUs, no money, no AI, no reputation.
- The **player** retains all knowledge. No in-game carry-over bonuses.
- The game's depth comes from *routing*: when do you stop freelancing and commit to SaaS? When do you invest vs. save? Which SaaS properties to max first?

### The Dashboard
After each completed run, the player sees:
- Run number
- Completion time
- Key split times (first $1K, $10K, $100K, $1M, $1B)
- Money source breakdown (freelance vs. MRR)

The dashboard persists across all runs.

### Intended run arc
| Run | Experience |
|---|---|
| Run 1 | Discovery. ~60–90 min. Player experiments, learns the loop. |
| Run 2 | Experimentation. Player tests routing (freelance-first vs. SaaS-first). ~30–40 min. |
| Run 3+ | Execution. Optimal route locked in. Sub-20 min possible for experts. |

## Open Design Questions

- What is the in-game time unit? (real minutes, or simulated "days"?) How fast do in-game days pass in real time?
- Should The Frontier Lab have a distinct visual identity within the Agents tab (dark theme, ominous branding) to contrast with the rest of the UI?
- Should downgrading a plan mid-run give a refund for the unused portion of the day, or is the daily cost always fully consumed?
- Should Customer Satisfaction, Retention, and Marketing Stream be shown as raw numbers or abstracted into letter grades (D → S)?
- Should Freelance Missions have a "rush" option — pay extra RCUs for double money — to add micro-decisions?
- Should the player name their SaaS product? (pure flavor, but adds attachment)
- Leaderboard: local only for now, online later?
- Should "Post on X" have compounding effects (a streak bonus for posting multiple days in a row)?
- Should agents have a visible "ROI indicator" so the player can tell at a glance whether their daily spend is net-positive?
